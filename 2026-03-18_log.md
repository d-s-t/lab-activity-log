## PCB Material Considerations
Regarding the PCB material, the important things are:
1. **Rigidity** (so that it doesn't shrink too much and tear off the wire bonds, disconnecting from the chip).
2. **Permittivity** ($\epsilon_r$ or Dk).
3. **Dissipation factor** ($\tan\delta$ or Df) [which I need to learn about.]

### The Effect of Dimensions and Permittivity
On one hand, the resonance frequencies of the box are affected like this:
$$f \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{\epsilon_r}}$$
So for high microwave frequencies, we would want a low $\epsilon_r$.

On the other hand, from what I understand, for a specific frequency:
$$\lambda \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{\epsilon_r}}$$
So in practice, it is possible to reduce the size of the circuit.
This has advantages in two aspects:
1. It is possible to design a smaller box; since the PCB does not fill the entire box, $\epsilon_{eff}$ will not affect it in the same way, and ultimately we will raise the resonance frequencies.
2. The trace width dictates the minimum distance between the connectors, and therefore increasing $\epsilon_r$ will increase the number of connectors per area.
